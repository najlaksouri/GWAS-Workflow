Heritability

Heritability measures, in a particular population, the proportion of variance of the phenotype that is due to genetic differences between individuals. (explica la propoci´on de la variación fenotipica que se debe a la variación genetica).

Broad sense heritability H2 is the ratio of total genetic variance to phenotypic variance H2 = VG/VP. On the other hand, narrow-sense heritability h2 = VA/VP, captures only that proportion of genetic variation that is due to additive genetic values (VA).

  • if H2 is high —–> then the phenotype is least influenced by the environment and selection for improvement of such character may be useful.

  • if H2 is low —–> then phenotype is highly influenced by the environment and genetic improvement through selection will be difficult. * Genetic advance GA** is the improvement in the mean genotype value of selected plants over the parental population. Genetic advance depends on i) Heritabilty and ii) selection intensity: means the proportion of individuals selected for the study, higher is better.

GA is the difference between phenotypic value of progeny and phenotypic value of the parentals.
